The 2026 TOD notification operates on a corridor-based approach — the entire Blue Line corridor within 500m on either side is the operative zone, covering approx. 207 sq. km citywide. Rajendra Place station sits well within this. Participation remains voluntary but FAR incentives (up to 500 vs. standard 250) are the key pull.

Planning verdict: Rajendra Place has exceptional transit and density credentials for TOD but faces a structural paradox — already-developed, fine-grained urban fabric makes large-scale TOD scheme assembly (≥1 ha) very difficult without public-sector land aggregation. The best TOD opportunities are along the Pusa Road frontage and in any DDA/public lands adjacent to the station.

05 — Planning Recommendations

10 Evidence-Based Recommendations

1
Transit Plaza + Multi-Modal Hub at the Metro Station
Redesign the area around Gate 2 (BLK Hospital side) and Gate 4 (Krishi Kunj) as pedestrian-priority transit plazas with e-rickshaw bays, cycle docking, weather shelter, and wayfinding. Integrate the upcoming DMRC Multi-Modal Integration (MMI) revamp with a street-level public realm improvement. The Pusa roundabout junction conflict (created by 2006 metro pillars) needs dedicated redesign with signal timing, pedestrian crossings, and removal of on-street parking within 100m of the station gates.
High priorityDMRC + DDAShort term
2
Managed Parking System — Multi-Level + Pricing
Develop 2–3 multi-level car parking (MLCP) structures on Pusa Road frontage plots. Implement priced on-street parking (pay-and-display or app-based) across the commercial core. Revenue should fund pedestrian infrastructure. This is a prerequisite for walkability: freeing pavement from cars is more impactful than any design intervention.
High priorityMCD + DDAShort–medium term
3
TOD Scheme on DDA/Public Land along Pusa Road
The key TOD barrier is land assembly. Identify all DDA-owned or government-held plots within 500m of the station — including Rajendra Bhawan, DMRC air rights, and any DDA surplus land — and prepare an Influence Zone Plan (IZP). Pusa Road has sufficient RoW for 18m road eligibility. A pilot 1-ha scheme combining affordable housing (65% FAR), commercial, and a public plaza would demonstrate the TOD policy at scale in a central Delhi District Centre context.
High priorityDDA + DMRCMedium term
4
Student Housing Regulation & PG Formalization
Rajendra Nagar's coaching economy drives a large, unregulated PG sector. Introduce a licensing regime for paying guest accommodations (as proposed under DDA's Rental Housing Policy in MPD-2041) with fire safety, occupancy, and rent disclosure norms. A dedicated "Student Zone" overlay in the Zonal Plan — permitting dormitory-style construction with appropriate FAR norms — would channel demand into safer, planned housing stock and reduce exploitation.
Medium priorityDDA + MCD + GNCTDMedium term
5
Ridge Buffer & Urban-Forest Interface Plan
The Central Ridge Protected Forest to the south is the locality's only ecological asset and groundwater recharge zone. Strengthen the buffer zone between Rajendra Nagar's southern edge and the Ridge with a hard demarcation (wall or planted berm), managed pedestrian access trails for recreation, and native species plantation (not vilayati kikar). Pursue the pending Forest Act Section 20 notification ordered by NGT (Jan 2025) to give the Ridge formal legal protection.
High priorityDelhi Forest Dept + NGTImmediate–medium term
6
Green Infrastructure & Urban Heat Mitigation
Central Delhi's commercial areas — including the Connaught Place–Karol Bagh belt — are identified as Delhi's highest urban heat island zones. For Rajendra Place: mandate tree-lined boulevards on all roads ≥9m RoW with a minimum canopy target; require green roofs or rooftop gardens on all new commercial buildings over 3 floors; develop pocket parks on any DDA surplus land. Trees can cool surroundings by the equivalent of a 7kW air conditioner; each tree planted is measurable urban resilience.
Medium priorityMCD + DDAAll phases
7
Active Street Frontage & Ground Floor Activation
MPD-2041 introduces "active frontage" norms for commercial areas and heritage zones. Rajendra Place's ground floors are dominated by parking podiums and blank walls. Mandate active uses (retail, cafes, bookstores — appropriate to the coaching economy) on all ground-floor commercial frontages along Pusa Road. Eliminate compound walls and setback dead zones. This alone transforms pedestrian experience.
Medium priorityDDA (ZDP revision)Medium term
8
Solid Waste & Decentralised Sanitation
High floating population (students, office workers) requires dense collection infrastructure. Install decentralised waste collection pods every 150–200m in the commercial core; introduce source segregation mandates for commercial establishments; and establish a bulk waste generator policy for coaching institutes (which produce large paper waste). The Pusa Road axis should have clean public toilet blocks as part of the TOD transit plaza design.
Medium priorityMCD + GNCTDShort term
9
Heritage & Identity — "Coaching District" Cultural Mapping
Rajendra Nagar is one of Delhi's most socially distinctive neighbourhoods — a planned Partition-era colony that has organically evolved into India's largest civil services preparation hub. MPD-2041 encourages cultural mapping and heritage overlays. A planning exercise to document and celebrate this identity — street art, wayfinding celebrating the coaching culture, bookstore clusters, study parks — would build a positive sense of place and resist generic gentrification pressures from TOD investment.
Low-medium priorityMCD + Civil Society
10
Integrated Governance — Multi-Agency Coordination Cell
Rajendra Place is governed by at least five agencies (DDA, MCD, DMRC, GNCTD, Delhi Forest Dept). The MPD-2041 TOD Committee (chaired by DDA Vice-Chairman) provides a model. A place-based coordination mechanism — an Area Management Committee for the Rajendra Place District Centre — with representatives from all agencies and civil society should be the planning unit for all interventions, replacing siloed departmental action.
Structural priorityAll agenciesImmediate
